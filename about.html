Who we are, what we cover, and why we cover it.

What Upstream is

Upstream is an independent blog covering open source software from the maintainer's side of the repository. Where most technology coverage focuses on the products built on top of open source, we focus on the projects themselves: how they're governed, how they're funded, how security issues get triaged, and what actually changes when a project outgrows its original maintainer.

What we cover

Our reporting is organized around five recurring themes:

  • Governance — how projects make decisions, transfer ownership, and formalize structure as they grow.
  • Funding — sponsorships, foundations, licensing changes, and the economics of sustaining a project long-term.
  • Security — disclosure processes, patch coordination, and the realities of triaging vulnerabilities in widely-depended-on code.
  • Community — maintainer burnout, contributor onboarding, and the human side of keeping a project alive.
  • Tooling — the build systems, CI pipelines, and infrastructure decisions that shape how a project ships.

Our approach

We write for developers and maintainers, not just about them. Articles are grounded in interviews, changelogs, and public project history rather than press releases, and we try to be specific about what changed, why, and what it cost — in engineering time, money, or both.
