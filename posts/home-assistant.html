Project Spotlight: Home Assistant — Local-First Smart Home Software Under Foundation Ownership

6 min read
← Back to all posts

Home Assistant was started in 2013 by Paulus Schoutsen, who wanted a smart home platform that didn't require sending data through a manufacturer's cloud service to control devices in his own house. That local-first, privacy-focused approach has remained the project's defining principle as it grew.

The commercial side of the project is run by Nabu Casa, a company Schoutsen co-founded that offers an optional paid cloud-connectivity service, while the core software itself has stayed free and open source under the Apache 2.0 license.

In April 2024, ownership of the Home Assistant source code and brand was transferred to the newly created Open Home Foundation, a nonprofit organized around three principles: privacy, choice, and sustainability. The foundation now owns and governs Home Assistant along with more than 240 related open source projects, standards, and libraries, explicitly to prevent any single company from controlling or abandoning them.

That governance structure was framed by the project as a safeguard rather than a change in direction — Schoutsen said at the time that it should mean no practical difference for developers or users, while giving the project independence from any single company's future decisions.
