Project Spotlight: Vue.js — A Framework Sustained by Sponsorship, Not a Company

5 min read
← Back to all posts

Vue.js was created by Evan You and first released in 2014, after he had worked with Angular at Google and wanted to extract the parts of it he found useful into something lighter weight and easier to adopt incrementally.

Unlike React (backed by Meta) or Angular (backed by Google), Vue was never the product of a large company's internal team. Its early growth came almost entirely through community adoption, particularly in parts of Asia and among developers looking for a gentler learning curve than its main alternatives.

That independence shaped how the project is funded: Evan You works on Vue full-time supported largely by sponsorships through platforms like GitHub Sponsors and Patreon, alongside funding channeled through Vue-related companies rather than a single corporate employer.

Vue is released under the MIT license, and its core team has since grown to include contributors working on the broader ecosystem — including Vite, the build tool You also created, which has been adopted well beyond Vue itself.
